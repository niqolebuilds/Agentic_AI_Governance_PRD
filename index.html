PROJECT VANGUARD · PRD

Adopting Singapore's MGF for Siloam's Agentic AI Governance

A simple operating model to deploy finance agents safely — bounded, accountable, controlled, understood.
Source framework: IMDA, "Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI" (Singapore). Adapted for Siloam Hospitals Group — Finance. Draft v1 · directional, pending CFO & Risk endorsement.
Why this matters for us: by the framework's own risk test (risk = likelihood × impact), Siloam's finance agents are high-risk by default — they touch financial transactions and sensitive personal/health data under UU PDP. So we don't start light. We start bounded.

1 · The model, in one glance — four pillars

1

Assess & bound risks upfront

Risk-tier each use case. Give agents least-privilege access and SOP-bound autonomy. Some tasks stay deterministic — not every job suits an agent.

2

Make humans accountable

Name who owns what. Put real human approval checkpoints at the stakes that warrant them — with override and feedback.

3

Technical controls & process

Separate sensitive data from the agent's context. Enforce limits by design, not by prompt. Tamper-proof audit trail. Test before, monitor after.

4

Enable end-user responsibility

Train the people who use and integrate agents to review outputs — not rubber-stamp them. AI output is a draft until a human owns it.

3 · The go-live gate — our short playbook

No agent goes live until it clears all seven. Print this; it's the whole governance in one card.
  1. Tiered. Risk-rated by likelihood × impact. Financial-transaction and PHI-touching agents = high tier → strongest controls.
  2. Bounded. Least-privilege access (only the tools/data it needs); autonomy constrained to a defined SOP; a kill-switch to take it offline.
  3. Owned. Named use-case owner · Risk sign-off · built by the CoE · SME approves outputs. All four roles assigned.
  4. Overseen. Human-approval checkpoints matched to the tier — with the power to override, plus a feedback loop. Match oversight to risk: escalation for high-volume recoverable tasks; human approval on every high-stakes output.
  5. Controlled by design. Sensitive PHI/financial data structurally separated from the agent's context; limits enforced by access control, not by prompt; a tamper-proof audit trail of every action.
  6. Tested. Tested before deployment (incl. red-team); continuously monitored and re-tested after.
  7. Understood. End users trained to review, not rubber-stamp — AI output is a draft until a human signs it.
